Introduction

What is The Collaboration Room?

You have been to networking events. You exchanged cards, made small talk, left with contacts you never followed up on. And somewhere between the handshakes and the pleasantries, the collaboration you actually needed never happened.

The Collaboration Room was built to fix that.

It is a curated work session — not a conference, not a panel, not a YouTube show, not an online event. It is a physical, in-person, deliberately structured gathering of 30 founders, builders, freelancers, techies and professionals in Lagos on June 20.

The entire session is designed around one outcome: real collaborations that begin before the event and keep moving long after it closes.

It was created by Miracle Oluwatayo Adekunle — known as Madam MO — a Product Manager and execution consultant at ActusBridge, driven by a belief that the right room with the right structure changes everything.

The Collaboration Room is where your idea meets the right person to build it with.

The Main Event

The Collaborator Auction — step by step

This is the centrepiece of June 20. Here is exactly how it works so you arrive prepared.

01
You come prepared with a real idea
Before the event, you identify one real idea, project or initiative you are working on — or want to start. It does not have to be fully formed. It just needs to be real and something you are genuinely ready to build with the right person.
02
You pitch to the room
You stand up and tell the room three things: what your idea is, what strengths you bring to it, and exactly where you need a collaborator — what skill, expertise or capacity is missing that would make this idea move.
03
Interested people bid
The room listens. People who see themselves as the right collaborator raise their hand and pitch why they should be the one to build with you. Not a casual "let's talk" — an actual pitch for the collaboration opportunity.
04
You choose who gets in
You review each person who bid and make your decision. The match is made deliberately, in a room full of witnesses, with the full context of what you are building and who you need beside you.
05
The top ideas get consulting support
The best two to three ideas from the auction receive hands-on support from Madam MO in collaboration with ActusBridge — covering product management and management consulting to help structure what comes next and ensure the collaboration keeps moving after the room closes.
Qualification

Who should — and should not — attend

This room is not for everyone. 30 seats means every person in the room needs to be the right person.

This is for you if —
  • You have an idea that has been waiting for the right person to build it with
  • You are a techie who can build anything but needs a business mind beside them
  • You are a freelancer ready to build something bigger than a client list
  • You have been building alone and have hit the ceiling of what one person can do
  • You are a professional ready to make the leap into entrepreneurship — but not alone
  • You have seen what real collaboration produces and are done settling for surface networking
  • You are ready to show up, pitch, and commit to building something with another person
This is not for you if —
  • You are still in the early idea stage and not ready to commit to building
  • You are looking for motivation, inspiration or general business advice
  • You are not ready to show up and do real work inside the room
  • You want to attend just to network casually without a clear intention to collaborate
  • You are not open to receiving feedback on your idea from a room of peers

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions people are asking

Is this a physical event, an online event, or a YouTube show?
The Collaboration Room is a fully physical, in-person event in Lagos, Nigeria on June 20, 2026. It is not a YouTube show, a webinar, or an online session. The format only works in person. That said, we will be running a series of videos and content on our Instagram page before and after the event — follow us so you do not miss out: @executewithactusbridge
How do I participate in the Collaborator Auction?
When you register, you are added to the community immediately and the matching process begins. You will receive a pre-event brief walking you through how to prepare your pitch — your idea, your strengths, and where you need a collaborator. On the day, you will have dedicated time to pitch to the room and receive bids from interested collaborators.
What if I do not have an idea to pitch? Can I still attend?
Yes. Not every attendee needs to pitch an idea. Some of the most valuable people in the room are the ones who show up as potential collaborators — ready to hear ideas and raise their hand for ones that align with their skills. If you are a builder, techie, strategist or creative looking for the right idea to attach yourself to, this room is equally for you.
What is the survey for?
The survey gathers insights from founders and builders across Africa to help us finalise the event experience. It is open to everyone — not just attendees — and takes less than 3 minutes. Share it freely.
What should I expect to leave with?
At minimum — a collaborator you chose deliberately in a room full of witnesses. Potentially — the beginning of a real project, partnership or business. If your idea is among the top ones from the auction, you also receive hands-on support from Madam MO in collaboration with ActusBridge to help structure what comes next.
Why is the venue and time not disclosed yet?
The venue and time will be shared exclusively with registered guests. This keeps the room curated and ensures only committed attendees know where to show up. Register to receive your full event details.
Can I get a refund if I register but cannot attend?
Tickets are non-refundable given the strictly capped capacity. However you may transfer your ticket to another person who meets the attendance criteria. Reach out via the contact details in this brochure to facilitate a transfer.
Who is Madam MO?
Madam MO is Miracle Oluwatayo Adekunle — a Product Manager at ActusBridge, operating across product management, management consulting, project management and execution strategy. She is also the founder of Empawr Hubs. The Collaboration Room was born from her belief that the right room, with the right people and the right structure, changes what is possible.
